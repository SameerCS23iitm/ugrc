
    Modbus Lacks Fundamental Security: Designed for isolated environments, Modbus protocols (especially Modbus TCP) lack encryption, authentication, access control, and integrity checks, making them highly vulnerable to cyberattacks.

    Common Vulnerabilities: Issues include default configurations, outdated firmware, absence of logging, susceptibility to replay, MitM, DoS, command injection, insider threats, and poor physical and network security.

    TLS Limitations for Modbus: Modbus TLS exists but is rarely practical due to legacy device limitations and the complexity of certificate management, especially in large-scale deployments.

    Mitigation Strategies: Best practices include strong authentication, encryption, regular updates, network segmentation, monitoring, employee training, and access control to reduce risk.

    Zero Trust with Veridify’s DOME: DOME provides 24/7 protection using a NIST-compliant Zero Trust model, enforcing mutual authentication, encrypting traffic, blocking unauthorized access, and securing legacy Modbus devices without requiring IT expertise.
